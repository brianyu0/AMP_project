Family

Massachusetts, United States February 19, 2015

Growing up I've been taught that family was always the most important. Today is Chinese New Year, and along with the food and praying at the altar, my family and I would all stand around the red paper furnace and burn joss paper. Every year we would pray to our deceased ancestors to wish them good virtue in the afterlife.

Chinese Holidays

Massachusetts, United States September 25, 2011

After moving back to the US, I had to learn English in first grade with the help of everyone's best friend, ESL! Although I do miss China and all of my cousins, I'm excited to make new friends in America. One part of China that I really enjoyed was all of the holidays. To name a few that I really liked is Chinese New Year (red envelopes) and the Mid-Autumn Festival. My parents would always give me $100 bills for new years.

The Mid-Autumn Festival always brought warm memories as the whole family would eat mooncakes and watch the Mid-Autumn Festival show.
